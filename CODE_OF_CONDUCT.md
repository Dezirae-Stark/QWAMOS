# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the QWAMOS project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

### Positive Behaviors

- **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people
- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
- **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
- **Showing patience and understanding** with contributors of varying skill levels
- **Welcoming new contributors** and helping them integrate into the community
- **Recognizing and crediting** the work and contributions of others
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language** in all communications
- **Respecting the time and effort** of maintainers and contributors

### Professional Conduct

- **Staying on topic** in discussions and issues
- **Providing constructive criticism** that is specific, actionable, and respectful
- **Respecting project decisions** made by maintainers, even if you disagree
- **Following established contribution guidelines** (see CONTRIBUTING.md)
- **Being mindful of security** when discussing vulnerabilities (see SECURITY.md)
- **Respecting intellectual property** and licensing requirements (AGPL-3.0)

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

### Harassment and Abuse

- The use of **sexualized language or imagery**, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- **Trolling**, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- **Public or private harassment** of any kind
- **Doxxing**: Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
- **Threats of violence** or incitement of violence towards any individual or group
- **Deliberate intimidation**, stalking, or following (online or in person)
- **Unwelcome sexual attention** or inappropriate physical contact

### Disruptive Behavior

- **Spamming** issues, pull requests, or discussions with irrelevant content
- **Deliberately derailing discussions** or creating off-topic noise
- **Repeatedly ignoring** maintainer requests or community guidelines
- **Disrupting project workflow** through intentional obstruction
- **Creating fake accounts** or impersonating other contributors
- **Plagiarizing code or documentation** without proper attribution

### Harmful Technical Conduct

- **Deliberately introducing malicious code** or security vulnerabilities
- **Sabotaging** the project through destructive commits or actions
- **Publicly disclosing security vulnerabilities** without following responsible disclosure (see SECURITY.md)
- **Attempting to bypass** security measures or access controls
- **Misusing project resources** (servers, CI/CD, etc.) for personal gain
- **Distributing modified versions** of QWAMOS that violate AGPL-3.0 license terms

### Other Unacceptable Conduct

- **Advocating for or encouraging** any of the above behaviors
- **Making sustained disruption** of community events or communications
- **Other conduct** which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### QWAMOS Project Maintainers

QWAMOS project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

**Maintainer Responsibilities**:

- **Review and moderate** all contributions, discussions, and community interactions
- **Remove, edit, or reject** comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- **Communicate reasons** for moderation decisions when appropriate
- **Apply enforcement guidelines** fairly and consistently
- **Escalate serious violations** to project leadership
- **Protect the privacy** of those reporting violations
- **Act in good faith** to uphold community standards

**Maintainer Authority**:

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- Make decisions about enforcement based on the severity and context of violations
- Seek input from other maintainers or community members when appropriate

**Maintainer Accountability**:

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all QWAMOS community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

### Community Spaces

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **GitHub repository**: Issues, pull requests, discussions, code comments, commits
- **Wiki pages**: All wiki contributions and edits
- **Communication channels**: Mailing lists, chat rooms, forums (when established)
- **Community events**: Virtual or in-person meetings, conferences, meetups
- **Social media**: When representing QWAMOS on Twitter, Mastodon, Reddit, etc.
- **Email communications**: Project-related emails to maintainers or contributors

### Representation

Examples of representing the QWAMOS community include:

- Using an official project e-mail address
- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event
- Wearing or displaying QWAMOS branding or materials
- Speaking on behalf of the project in public forums or media

### External Interactions

While this Code of Conduct primarily governs behavior within QWAMOS community spaces, serious violations occurring outside these spaces may be considered if they impact the safety or well-being of community members or the project's reputation.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the QWAMOS project maintainers responsible for enforcement at:

**Email**: [qwamos@tutanota.com](mailto:qwamos@tutanota.com)

**Subject Line**: `[CODE OF CONDUCT] Brief description of incident`

### What to Include in Your Report

To help us address the situation effectively, please include:

1. **Your contact information** (for follow-up)
2. **Names (usernames) of individuals involved**
3. **Date and time** of the incident (with timezone if possible)
4. **Description of the incident**: What happened, in as much detail as you can provide
5. **Context**: Where the incident occurred (issue, PR, discussion, etc.)
6. **Links or screenshots**: Evidence of the behavior (if available)
7. **Witnesses**: Names of anyone else who witnessed the incident
8. **Impact**: How the behavior affected you or others
9. **Previous incidents**: Whether this is part of an ongoing pattern

### Confidentiality

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All project maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

**Privacy Protections**:

- Reporter identity will be kept confidential to the extent possible
- Details will only be shared with those necessary for investigation
- No retaliation will be tolerated against reporters
- Anonymous reports will be accepted and investigated to the best of our ability

### Response Timeline

- **Initial acknowledgment**: Within 48 hours
- **Investigation**: 5-10 days (may vary based on complexity)
- **Decision**: Within 14 days of initial report
- **Appeal**: Decisions may be appealed within 7 days

## Enforcement Guidelines

QWAMOS project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from project maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Example Violations**:
- Using mildly inappropriate language
- Making an unintentionally insensitive comment
- First-time minor violations without malicious intent

**Typical Duration**: Immediate correction, no ongoing consequences if behavior improves

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example Violations**:
- Repeated inappropriate language after correction
- Making disrespectful comments about others' work
- Minor harassment or disruptive behavior
- Ignoring maintainer guidance after correction

**Typical Duration**: 30 days of restricted interaction

**Restrictions**:
- No commenting on issues or pull requests
- No participation in discussions
- No direct messages to community members
- No social media interaction related to the project

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**Example Violations**:
- Sustained harassment or disruptive behavior
- Publicly disclosing private information (doxxing)
- Repeated violations after warning
- Deliberately introducing malicious code
- Serious threats or aggressive behavior

**Typical Duration**: 90 days to 1 year, depending on severity

**Restrictions**:
- Complete ban from all community spaces
- No contributions accepted (code, issues, discussions, wiki)
- No communication with community members
- GitHub account may be blocked from repository

**Reinstatement**:
- Must demonstrate understanding of violation
- Must commit to following Code of Conduct
- Must be approved by project maintainers

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Example Violations**:
- Severe or repeated harassment
- Threats of violence or actual violence
- Doxxing with malicious intent
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Sustained disruption after temporary ban
- Distribution of malware or intentional sabotage
- Pattern of violations showing no intent to improve
- Legal violations (e.g., copyright infringement, hacking)

**Consequence Details**:
- Permanent removal from all community spaces
- All contributions may be removed or attributed to "Anonymous"
- GitHub account permanently blocked
- No possibility of reinstatement
- May be reported to GitHub and/or law enforcement if illegal activity

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

If you believe a Code of Conduct decision was made in error, you may appeal by:

1. **Sending an email** to [qwamos@tutanota.com](mailto:qwamos@tutanota.com)
2. **Subject line**: `[CODE OF CONDUCT APPEAL] Your Name`
3. **Within 7 days** of the original decision
4. **Include**:
   - Original decision and date
   - Reason you believe the decision was incorrect
   - Any additional context or evidence
   - What outcome you're requesting

### Appeal Review

- Appeals will be reviewed by project maintainers not involved in the original decision
- Response will be provided within 14 days
- Decision on appeal is final
- Frivolous appeals may result in additional consequences
